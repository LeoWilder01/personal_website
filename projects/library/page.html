Design · 2022

How to Read a Library

A spatial medium that transcends information access — a space that becomes its own form of knowledge.

Date 2022.03 – 2022.05 Type Personal Project Feature Architectural design · Media theory · Spatial cognition

Background

The internet and electronic devices are gradually replacing print media as the primary means for people to access information. Meanwhile, the influence of physical architectural spaces and the sensory experiences they convey has diminished. As information exchange and knowledge interaction increasingly occur in the digital realm, a disconnect has emerged in how people navigate life between the physical and virtual worlds.

Approach

The influence of the medium's form surpasses that of its content — McLuhan. The formal characteristics of a medium, when repeatedly manifested under different artificial environments and material conditions, constitute its greatest social impact. This project aims to design a spatial medium that goes beyond serving as a space for accessing information. Instead, it becomes a new form of "information" that can be read in its own right — offering not traditional knowledge, but a conceptual understanding of spatial functionality and a redefined perception of space.
